How We Built These Numbers

Every number on this dashboard traces to a specific data source and a model that was backtested against actual financials. Nothing is estimated from averages alone.

Data Sources

  • Toast POS system via Orders API — daily revenue actuals by restaurant. Revenue = checks[].amount (pre-tax, pre-tip net sales matching P&L "Total Sales" line).
  • Tock Reservation system via Daily Export API — forward bookings, party sizes, booking creation timestamps. 118,947 C&S reservations, 79,588 Jolie, 72,360 Brodeto, 30,819 CB.
  • P&L Monthly Profit & Loss statements from QuickBooks — used for backtesting and revenue-per-cover calibration.

The RTD + RTG Model

Projected Revenue = Revenue to Date (actuals) + Revenue to Go (booked + future reservations)

Revenue to Date (RTD) — Actual daily revenue from Toast for completed days. Each order fetched individually via the Toast Orders v2 API, revenue summed from non-voided checks.

Revenue to Go (RTG) has two components:

  • Booked reservations — Current Tock bookings for future dates, multiplied by calibrated revenue-per-cover for that day of week.
  • Future reservations — Reservations that haven't been made yet but will come in based on historical build curve patterns. Uses a 1.0x cap against historical average daily reservations per day-of-week.

Build Curves

Every day of the week has its own booking pattern. We analyzed 10 months of steady-state reservation data (June 2025 - February 2026) to learn how bookings accumulate over time. Example for Brodeto:

Days Before ServiceSaturdayFridayTuesday
28 days11%9%4%
14 days26%20%14%
7 days41%34%26%
3 days55%49%40%
1 day63%61%49%
Day of73%71%61%

This means even on the day of service, roughly 30% of reservations were made that same day. The model accounts for this by projecting how many more bookings will come in based on where we are on the curve.

Revenue Per Cover (Calibrated)

Base revenue-per-cover comes from Toast daily actuals (Week 1 April 2026), then adjusted upward based on the gap between Toast checks[].amount and P&L Total Sales. The adjustment accounts for service charges and other items that appear in the P&L but not in Toast's check subtotal.

Walk-in Analysis

Calibration comparing Tock covers vs Toast guests for April 1-7 showed these are reservation-only restaurants. Tock covers meet or exceed Toast guest counts on every day tested. Walk-in rate is effectively 0%, which simplifies the model — all revenue flows through reservations.

March 2026 Backtest

We validated the model by pretending it was March 10 and projecting March revenue, then comparing against actual P&L results:

RestaurantModel ProjectedActual P&LError
Crawford & Son$145,984$151,772-3.8%

The model tends to slightly underproject for 5-night restaurants and slightly overproject for 7-night restaurants. This conservative bias is intentional — it's better to beat a projection than miss one.

Labor Tracking Model

Labor data comes from the Toast Labor API (/labor/v1/timeEntries), which reports hourly wages, overtime, and employee counts per day. However, Toast only captures hourly wages — not salaried managers, payroll taxes, benefits, or management fees that appear on the P&L.

Estimated Actual Payroll = Toast API Wages x Multiplier

The multiplier bridges the gap between API-reported wages and total P&L payroll:

Restaurant TypeMultiplierDerivation
5-night (C&S, Jolie)2.48xMarch P&L payroll / Toast API wages for same period
7-night (Brodeto, CB)1.89xSame methodology — lower multiplier because 7-night has more hourly coverage

The 30% labor target is the bonus threshold from the Manager Reports. In March 2026, no restaurant hit 30%: C&S was 34.2%, Jolie 32.9%, Brodeto 33.0%. This is consistent across the portfolio — labor % is primarily a revenue problem, not a cost problem. Payroll varies 7-15% month to month, while revenue varies 25-64%.

Key Limitations

  • Individual day projections can swing 30-40% from actual. The model converges at the month level, not the day level.
  • Revenue-per-cover is based on one week of Toast data (April 1-7). More weeks will improve accuracy.
  • Crawford Brothers has no March P&L yet for backtesting — using Brodeto's calibration parameters.
  • Projections for dates 14+ days out are less reliable because fewer bookings are in place. Accuracy improves daily as the booking window closes.
  • The model improves every day: as more days become actuals, RTD grows and RTG (the uncertain part) shrinks.

What Makes This Self-Learning

Each completed month provides a new backtest. We compare what the model projected vs what actually happened, identify where errors concentrate (which day-of-week, which distance out), and adjust parameters. The March backtest already taught us:

  • 1.0x cap outperforms 1.2x and 1.5x caps on build curve projections
  • Toast revenue-per-cover needs a $3-5 upward adjustment to match P&L net sales
  • 5-night restaurants need the RPC adjustment more than 7-night restaurants
